Workers inspect pork innards at a meat-packing plant….

“Workers inspect pork innards at a meat-packing plant. Upton Sinclair brought the conditions of such plants into public view in his novel The Jungle.”

  • In this photograph, workers from the Gilded age can be seen working in a meat-packing factory with no sanitary precautions. This was before the Meat inspection Act of 1906. The workers are not wearing gloves when handling the meat. Workers can be seen without any clothing protection. Workers also have little room to maneuver. This was before the Meat inspection Act of 1906.
  • As the title says, conditions like this inspired the novel The Jungle by Upton Sinclair. In this novel, he describes the horrible conditions. He describes the workers’ fingers being grounded up and mixed with the meat. In addition to that, he described how the machinery leaves the workers’ fingers as nubs and how the acid eats away their hand.

Room in a Tenement (Jacob Riis, 1890)

In the photograph a family of seven are living in a small cramped room that would normally be used for only one person. The room is in a very poor condition, it has a kitchen, beds and closet in just one room. The room looks very disheveled and the room has no windows to accommodate sunlight.

The apartments that the lower-working class could afford were never repaired by the by the landlords. This left the people with broken and shamble home with little heat for the winters. Some tenements contains up to 12 people in the small, unventilated room. These tenements were dirty and known to spread diseases.

HARRIS & EWING/UNDERWOOD ARCHIVES/GETTY IMAGES (1908)

“This 1908 photo shows, from left to right, steel mogul Andrew Carnegie, politician William Jennings Bryan, financier James J. Hill, and labor leader John Mitchell.”

  • This picture shows the Steel mogul Andrew Carnegie and the financier James J. Hill. They are pictured with politician William Jennings Bryan and labor John Mitchell. Both Carnegie and Hill were known as “Robber Barons” for their exploitative business practices.
  • This photograph is shown through the political and social lens because Carnegie is a steel mongul and owner of a monopoly and advocates for laborers. It is showing two opposing sides, one being for the rich elite while the other for the common people.
  • Andrew Carnegie was known as a “Robber Baron” because his business practices. His factories were firing union workers for non-union workers. “Butchers at the Deli”(Aqib Jalees 2022)

This is a photograph of a current, modern day butcher shop/meat shop where a man can be seen chopping a goat into particular pieces with his supervisor closely monitoring him. The photograph is shown in a social lens in the way that health regulations have severally upgraded compared to back in the Gilded Age as back then not even gloves were used, in the present the man in the picture is using gloves, wearing clothing to both protect himself and keep clean, and a mask to prevent the spread of covid

These precautions like the gloves and coat were added by the Meat Inspection Act. This insures that all meats are sanitary before being packed and sold.

“Modern Day Apartments” (Aqib Jalees, 2022)

This is a photograph of a current, modern day one bathroom, two bedroom, one kitchen, one living room apartment with ample closets. (The tenements of the gilded age didn’t even have separate rooms or running water.) This is a big space for a small family to comfortably live in with a lot of hygienic and sanitary

measurements. This photograph is shown in a social and economic lens because it shows the modern day apartment, compared to gilded age tenements, the modern apartment is considerably larger and well kept, however, it is still incredibly expensive compared to what you get, similar to a Gilded Age tenement.

“Donald Trump, Michael Bloomberg and Jared Kushner Attend the New York Observer’s 25th Anniversary in New York in 2013.”

This photograph is an image that depicts some of the biggest and most famous political figures/people in the modern day. From left to right Donald Trump, Michael Bloomberg, and Jared Kushner.
